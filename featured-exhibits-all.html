Nintendo Family Computer

The Nintendo Family Computer (Famicom) was released in Japan on July 15, 1983. It was Nintendo’s first home console, featuring 8-bit graphics and interchangeable game cartridges.
First games included Donkey Kong, Donkey Kong Jr., and Popeye: These arcade classics helped boost its popularity.
Global Expansion: The Famicom was redesigned and released in North America as the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) in 1985. Discontinued in Japan (2003): After 20 years, Nintendo officially stopped production.

Year: 1983-2003

BellSouth

BellSouth, established in 1983, was a major telecommunications provider in the Southeastern United States, offering landline, mobile, and internet services. It remained a dominant player until its acquisition by AT&T in 2006, marking the end of its independent operations.

Year: 1983-2006

Nintendo Gun Controller

The Nintendo Gun Controller, known as the NES Zapper, was first released in Japan on February 18, 1984, for the Famicom. It later launched in North America on October 18, 1985, alongside the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES).
It was primarily used for light gun games like Duck Hunt, Wild Gunman, and Hogan’s Alley. The original Japanese version resembled a real revolver, but the North American version was redesigned to look more futuristic. In 1988, an orange version was introduced to comply with toy gun regulations

Year: 1984

Nintendo Entertainment System (NES)

The global release of Nintendo's iconic gaming console that shaped the industry. Released in North America on October 18, 1985, the NES played a crucial role in reviving the video game industry after the 1983 crash. The Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) was officially discontinued in North America and Europe on August 14, 1995

Year: 1985 - 1995

Nintendo Wireless Family Computer

The Nintendo Wireless Family Computer with an antenna was released in the Philippines in 1986, shortly after the EDSA Revolution. It was part of Nintendo’s Family Computer (Famicom) lineup, which was popular in Japan and other regions.
The Nintendo Wireless Family Computer was discontinued along with the original Famicom in Japan on September 25, 2003. This marked the end of its production after 20 years of service.

Year: 1986-2003
