Other Exhibit

The Other Exhibit showcases a collection of historical tech milestones, including communication platforms, retail spaces, and internet services that shaped digital culture in the late 1990s to present.

 

BellSouth

A major telecommunications company that operated from 1983 until its merger with AT&T in 2006.

Year: 1983-2006

Dial-Up Screen

Commonly used during the 1990s and early 2000s, dial-up internet required a modem and phone line to connect online.

Year: 1990s-late 2000s

Tower Records Makati

An iconic music store in Makati that catered to audiophiles before shutting down in the mid-2000s.

Year: 1990s-mid-2000s

Internet Yellow Pages

A printed directory from 1995, listing websites before search engines became widely used.

Year: 1995

ISP Bonanza

A prepaid internet service in the Philippines during the late 1990s and early 2000s.

Year: 1996

CD-R King Shop

A budget-friendly electronics store that peaked in popularity before closing in 2020.

Year: 1997-2020

AOL Messenger

One of the earliest instant messaging platforms, widely used in the late 90s and early 2000s.

Year: 1997

Yahoo Messenger

A popular instant messaging platform with chat rooms, file sharing, and voice calls.

Year: 1998

RSS (Really Simple Syndication)

Introduced in 1999, RSS allowed users to subscribe to website updates and automatically receive content feeds.

Year: 1999

MSN Messenger

Microsoft’s instant messaging service, later rebranded as Windows Live Messenger.

Year: 1999

Napster

The first peer-to-peer music-sharing service, sparking a revolution in digital music distribution.

Year: 1999

EDSAMail

A Philippine-based email service that operated in the early 2000s, offering free and paid accounts with built-in ads.

Year: 2000-2005

SBC Yahoo!

A collaboration between SBC Communications and Yahoo, providing broadband internet services until it transitioned to AT&T Yahoo! in 2005.

Year: 2000s-2005

LimeWire

A peer-to-peer file-sharing software used for downloading music and other media.

Year: 2000

Skype

The pioneering video chat and voice call service that revolutionized digital communication.

Year: 2003
